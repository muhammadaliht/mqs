Overview of Salah (How to perform Salah)

Salah (daily prayer) is one of Islam's five pillars and a vital act of worship. It is performed five times daily at specific times: Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha. It is a direct link between a believer and Allah. Salah promotes devotion, discipline, and mindfulness while reminding Muslims of their purpose.

To learn how to pray Salah, you need to know how to do steps like ablution (Wudu), the right direction (Qibla), and the right intention (Niyyah). During the prayer, certain body moves are made, such as standing (Qiyam), bowing (Ruku), prostrating (Sujood), and sitting (Tashahhud). Quranic recitations, such as Surah Al-Fatiha and other verses from the Quran, are also recited.

Every part of Salah has a deep meaning that promotes humility, thanks, and devotion. For beginners, it's essential to start with the basics, like slowly learning the words and movements, before getting better at the more minor details. This guide will help you through the process by explaining the steps and meanings of Salah. This will help you build a regular and fulfilling prayer practice.

How to Pray Namaz Step By Step

Here is a simple, step-by-step guide to pray Namaz (Salah):

Preparation

Do Wudu (ablution) and make sure you are clean.
Face the Qibla, which is the direction of Mecca's Kaaba.
Wear modest clothing and stand in a clean place.

Start the Salah

Niyyah (Intention): Make a silent intention to pray.
Raise your hands and say "Allahu Akbar," which means "Allah is Greatest." It's called Takbir.

Qiyam (Standing)

Place your right hand over your left on the navel.
Recite Surah Al-Fatiha, then another Surah or part of the Quran.

Ruku (Bowing)

Bow down and put your hands on your knees.
Say Subhana Rabbiyal Azeem (Glory to My Lord, the Most Great) three times.

Qooma/I'tidal (Standing upright)

Say "Sami' Allahu liman Hamidah," which means "Allah hears those who praise Him."
Say, Rabbana Lakal Hamd (Our Lord, all praise is due to You)

Sujud (Prostration)

Go down, rest your forehead on the ground, and repeat Subhana Rabbiyal A'la (Glory to my Lord, the Most High) three times.

Jalsa (sitting between the two Sujuds)

In Namaz, Jalsa is the sitting position between the two Sujud (prostrations), where one rests a little while praying.

Second Sujud

In Namaz, the Second Sujud is when you prostrate again after the Jalsa, this time putting your face on the ground and praising Allah.

Repeat for Each Rak'ah

Repeat steps 3–6 for 2, 3, or 4 Rak'ahs depending on the prayer.

Tashahhud (Sitting for Testimony)

Recite the Tashahhud, Salawat, and other prayers at the last sitting.

Salam (End of Prayer)

First, turn your head to the right and say, "Assalamu Alaikum wa Rahmatullah," then to the left.

Types of Namaz: How to Pray Salah

Different types of salah (prayer) in Islam are classified according to their obligation and purpose. The main types of Salah are listed below:

Fardh (Obligatory Prayers)

Every Muslim has to perform these prayers every day; skipping them is a sin. Five Fardh prayers are offered every day:

Fajr (dawn): 2 Rak’ahs
Zuhr (early afternoon): 4 Rak'ahs
Asr (late afternoon): 4 Rak'ahs
Maghrib (after sunset): 3 Rak’ahs
Isha (night): 4 Rak’ahs

Fardh prayers are vital in Islam, and performing them is a direct commandment from Allah.

Wajib (Compulsory but Slightly Lower Than Fardh)

Although Wajib prayers are also obligatory, skipping them without a valid excuse is not good.

Witr: prayer after Isha.
Eid Salah.

Sunnah (Salah of Prophet)

Even though these prayers aren't obligatory, Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) regularly performed them. There are two kinds of Sunnah prayers:

Sunnah Mu'akkadah (Emphasized): Highly recommended, such as two Rak'ahs before Fajr and four before Zuhr.
Sunnah Ghair Mu'akkadah (Non-Emphasized):Performed sometimes, like extra prayers before Asr.

Nafil (Voluntary Prayers)

These are extra prayers that are performed to get extra benefits. They include:

Tahajjud: Prayed late at night before Fajr.
Ishraq: 15-20 minutes after sunrise.
Duha/Chashat: Prayed after sunrise but before Zuhr.
Istikhara: Prayed for asking Allah's guidance when making decisions.

Janazah (Funeral Prayer)

This is a prayer for all Muslims who have died. It is a communal obligation (Fardh Kifayah), meaning everyone must ensure it is performed.

Other Prayers


Salat al Kusuf (Eclipse Prayer): Performed during a solar or lunar eclipse.
Salat al Istisqa (Rain Prayer): Prayed in times of drought, begging Allah for rain.

Muslims can improve their relationship with Allah through different types of worship, as every kind of Salah has benefits and meanings.

How to Perform Salah Women: How to Pray Salah for Female

Here is a step-by-step guide for women to pray Salah:

Preparation

Purification: Perform Wudu (ablution).
Dress Code: Cover your entire body with loose, modest clothes, including your hair, with a scarf so that your face and hands are visible.

Start the Prayer

Niyyah (Intention): Make a silent intention to pray
Raise your hands to shoulder level and say "Allahu Akbar," which means "Allah is Greatest." It's called Takbir.

Qiyam (Standing)

Place your right hand over the left on your chest.
Recite Surah Al-Fatiha, then another Surah or part of the Quran.

Ruku (Bowing)

Put your hands on your knees without bending your arms and bow slightly (don't bend like males).
Say Subhana Rabbiyal Azeem (Glory to My Lord, the Most Great) three times.

Qooma/I'tidal (Standing Upright)

Say "Sami' Allahu liman Hamidah," which means "Allah hears those who praise Him."
Say, Rabbana Lakal Hamd (Our Lord, all praise is due to You)

Sujud (Prostration)

Place your face, nose, palms, knees, and toes on the ground to prostrate and repeat Subhana Rabbiyal A'la (Glory to my Lord, the Most High) three times.

Jalsa (sitting between the two Sujuds)

In Namaz, Jalsa is the sitting position between the two Sujud (prostrations), where one rests a little while praying.

Second Sujud

In Namaz, the Second Sujud is when you prostrate again after the Jalsa, this time putting your face on the ground and praising Allah.

Repeat for each rak'ah.

Repeat steps for 2, 3, or 4 Rak'ahs depending on the prayer.

Tashahhud (Sitting for Testimony)

Recite the Tashahhud, Salawat, and other prayers at the last sitting.

Salam (End of Prayer)

First, turn your head to the right and say, "Assalamu Alaikum wa Rahmatullah," then to the left.

The steps are the same as for men's Salah, but there is a slight difference in some postures, such as Ruku and Sujud. To be more modest, you should sit more closely and keep your arms closer to your body during Sujud.

Difference Between Male and Female Prayer

According to the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), the differences between male and female prayer (Namaz) in Islam are based on particular postures, movements, and levels of modesty. The differences between each part of the prayer are listed below:

Qiyam (Standing)


Male
Place feet about shoulder-width and stand straight up.
During Takbir (saying "Allahu Akbar"), hands are raised to the level of the ears or just above them.
Female
Stand straight up with her feet closer together to look more humble.
During Takbir, hands are raised to chest level.

Hands Positioning (During Qiyam)


Male
Place the right hand over the left and place on the navel.
Female
Puts the right hand over the left hand higher on the chest, close to the breast area, to keep it hidden.

Recitation (Qirat)


Male
When performing with Jamat, recite aloud in some prayers (e.g., Fajr, Maghrib, and Isha).
Recite silently in other prayers (e.g., Dhuhr and Asr).
Female
Recite silently in all prayers, even when alone, to maintain modesty.

Ruku (bowing)


Male
Keep the back parallel and straight with the ground level while bending at a 90-degree angle.
The elbows are kept away from the body, making a wide stance.
Female
Bend less and maintain a more modest stance.
The elbows are placed close to the sides, minimizing the width of the stance.

Sujood (Prostration)


Male
Place the nose and forehead on the ground while lifting the elbows away from the ground.
The knees and feet are spaced apart, making a wider stance.
Female
The nose and forehead are on the ground, and the body is more petite.
The legs are kept close together, and the elbows are placed close to the sides for modesty.

Jalsa and Tashahhud (Sitting Between Prostrations and Final Sitting)


Male
Place the left foot flat on the ground and the right foot straight up, with the toes pointing towards the Qibla.
The hands are on the thighs, and the fingers are spread out slightly.
Female
Sit down on their left hip with both legs bent to the side.
Hands are put gently on the legs, but the fingers are not spread out.

Salam (End of Prayer)


Male
Move the head to the right and then to the left while saying, "Assalamu Alaikum wa Rahmatullah."
Female
Turn the head gently with smaller movements to maintain modesty and say, "Assalamu Alaikum wa Rahmatullah."

Overall Etiquette and Motions


Male
Movements are wider and more visible.
The clothing is loose and facilitates a complete range of motion.
Female
Movements are gentle, close, and reserved.
Clothes like a scarf or hijab cover the whole body, emphasizing privacy and modesty.
