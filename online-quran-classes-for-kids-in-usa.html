Learning the Correct Way To Pronounce and Write the Arabic Language

Learning the Arabic alphabet and how to pronounce words is one of the most important things you need to do to master the Quranic language. The first step is to teach kids the Arabic script, which has 28 letters, each with its own sound. Learning how to say these letters correctly is very important because even a tiny mistake in speech can change what words mean in Arabic. During this time, kids learn to recognize and say each letter through enjoyable activities, such as using visual aids and phonetic exercises and doing the same thing repeatedly.

Then, the focus moves to vowel sounds (Harakat) and other vital signs for pronunciation, like Sukoon (a letter that isn't spoken) and Shaddah (a letter that is doubled). Not only is this essential information necessary to read the Quran, but it's also necessary to talk and understand Arabic. Children prepare for a deeper and more accurate study of the Quran and Islam by learning the Arabic language and how to say its letters correctly.

Rules of Tajweed

Following Tajweed rules when learning the Quran is essential to a child's Islamic schooling. Tajweed, the art of reciting the Quran with the right tone and pronunciation, is needed to keep the text's beauty and truth. When kids learn the Quran according to Tajweed rules, they get better at reciting it and knowing what Allah wants them to do.

To do this, you need to know essential things like Makharij al-Huruf (articulation points), Sifaat-ul-Huruf (characteristics of letters), and how to use the Nun Sakinah and Tanween rules. As an essential part of Tajweed, you should also know how to correctly use Madd (elongation) methods and Qalqalah (echo sound). By teaching these rules along with the Quran from a young age, children learn to respect and love the Quran's precise language deeply. This ensures that future generations will continue the tradition of reading the Quran correctly and with sincerity.
