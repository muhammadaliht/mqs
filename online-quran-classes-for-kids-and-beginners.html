Beginner, Intermediate, and Advanced Quran Classes for Kids.

There are different levels of Quran classes for children to meet their needs and skill levels. Let's look at the three critical levels: basic, advanced, and advanced.

Beginner Level

At the beginning level, kids learn the basics of reading from the Quran. First, they learn the Arabic alphabet and how to say each letter correctly. They learn simple words and short sentences from the Quran as they go along. If they start at this level, they will have a strong understanding of the Quran as they grow.

Intermediate Level

Kids build on what they learned in the starting stage as they move on to the intermediate level. They learn new words and get better at scanning Arabic text. Through translation tasks, they also start to understand what the verses mean on a basic level. At this stage, they can learn more about the teachings of the Quran and feel more confident in their ability to recite it.

Advance Level

At the intermediate level, kids learn more about the Quran's more complicated ideas. They know how to tafsir (interpret) challenging verses and discuss the moral lessons that different chapters teach. Learning longer surahs or whole juz (parts) of the Quran may also be a goal for more advanced students. Daily Prayers / Masnoon Duas

Daily prayers, known as dua in Arabic, are essential to Muslim's day. These prayers help ask for guidance, blessings, & from Allah. Muslims say different duas for many situations. For example, there's one before sleeping and another after waking. They also have duas for before eating and after finishing a meal and ones for entering and leaving their homes.

Remember the duas for going in and out of the bathroom and ones for drinking water & milk! There are also special duas for going upstairs or downstairs. These prayers help Muslims show thankfulness, ask for help, and keep a strong bond with Allah daily. By adding du'as into their daily habits, Muslims try to remember their faith, look for Allah's mercy, & feel peace and strength inside.

Benefits of Learning Daily Duas

Kids' daily supplication (Masnoon Duas) course is a great way to teach them Islamic ideals and help them build a strong relationship with Allah. This course teaches kids the most important duas for everyday things like getting up, eating, and going to bed. This helps them make remembering Allah a part of their daily lives. Children learn these prayers in an organized way that allows them to understand what they mean, which gives their spiritual journey meaning.

Kids will enjoy and be interested in interactive daily supplication lessons. Children learn real duas from the Quran and Sunnah through individualised lessons taught by trained teachers. Using audiovisual tools and repeating things helps you remember them, which makes learning easier and more pleasant.

Namaz / Salah

Namaz, which many call Salah, is a key form of worship (Ibadah) and Islam's initial fardh (obligation). The Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him and his progeny) said: "The first thing that Allah made obligatory upon my Ummah was the five prayers, and the first thing from their acts of worship that shall be taken up will be the five prayers." Namaz is how Muslims connect with Allah to ask for guidance, peace, and blessings.

Salah includes a mix of physical & spiritual actions. It has recitations (Tilawat, Qirat, Fatihah) from the Quran and specific movements. These movements are standing (Qayam, Takbir), bowing (Ruku), prostrating (Sujud), & sitting (Tashahud, Ata'hayyat). The five daily prayers—Fajr (Two Rakah), Dhuhr (Four Rakah), Asr (Four Rakah), Maghrib (Three Rakah), and Isha (Four Rakah)—are a vital part of a Muslim's day. They remind us how important faith & devotion are. It is necessary to perform ablution before prayer.

6 Kalimay

Kalimah means "word" in Arabic. It often refers to the critical declaration of faith called the Shahada. The kalima لَآ اِلٰهَ اِلَّا اﷲُ مُحَمَّدٌ رَّسُوْلُ اﷲِ means that “Nobody is worthy of worship except Allah & Muhammad is His Messenger”. The Shahada shows that there is no God but Allah & Muhammad is His Messenger. This statement is the central belief in Islam—showing that Allah is one & Muhammad is a prophet. Islam has six kalimas: the first kalma, second kalma, third kalma, fourth kalma, fifth kalma, & sixth kalma. Saying these Kalimay is super crucial for Muslims; it proves their acceptance of Islam & their commitment to its beliefs. The Shahada plays a significant role in Islamic teachings & acts as a unifying declaration for Muslims worldwide.

The Kalimah in Islam, additionally called the Shahada, holds extensive importance because of the announcement of faith for Muslims. It's far a concise affirmation of the precious beliefs in Islam, اَشْهَدُ اَنْ لَّآ اِلٰهَ اِلَّا اللهُ وَحْدَہٗ لَاشَرِيْكَ لَہٗ وَاَشْهَدُ اَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهٗ وَرَسُولُہٗ pointing out that There is no God but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger. This declaration of religion serves as the cornerstone of Islamic perception, emphasizing Allah's oneness and Muhammad's prophethood (Peace be upon Him). With the valuable resource of reciting the Kalimah (I endure witness that there is none worthy of worship except Allah and that I undergo witness that Muhammad is His servant and his Messenger), Muslims are unique in their commitment to monotheism and the teachings of Islam, uniting believers globally underneath this essential creed.
