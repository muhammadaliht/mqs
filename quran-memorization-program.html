Understanding the Structure of the Quran in the Online Hifz Program

The sacred book of Islam, the Quran, is a masterpiece that guides Muslims worldwide. Its thirty equal portions, called "Parahs" or "Juz" (plural: "Ajza"), make memorization and recitation simpler. The number of chapters (Surahs) and verses (Ayahs) in each Juz varies, and they cover a wide range of subjects, lessons, and events. The significance and content of each of the thirty Parahs of the Quran are examined in detail here.

The Quran is a literary masterpiece and spiritual guidance. It is divided into 114 surahs, each of different lengths and not in chronological order. Instead, they are arranged according to the Surah's length, with the longest usually taking place at the start and the shortest near the finish. The Quran's thirty juz allow Muslims to complete the reading in a month, especially during Ramadan, when many aim to recite the whole book.
